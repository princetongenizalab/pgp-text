English Translation of Letter: T-S AS 153.128

Alan Elbaum, unpublished editions (n.p., 2022).

recto

  1. Yeshuʿa with . . . gratitude for the favor
  2. of the lord toward his servant with his dear letter.
  3. That which the lord mentioned . . . that it would be prudent to
  4. fulfill(?) my letter and not show it, I fear that he/this will make me resort to another, sterner letter
  5. . . . unless they have reached a settlement,
  6. which I doubt. Tha[t which I desir]e from my lord is that you
  7. continue to be active in your [beneficial?] speech to al-Akram Abū l-Rabīʿ,
  8. and inform him that if he should leave Fustat/Egypt without the case having been resolved
  9. and having something in his hand—or each one of them writing in his hand concerning. . .—then he has not carried out the task,
  10. and he will constrain me to act in their. . . many times what I had. . . .

recto, right margin

  1. The lord should forgive me: 
  2. I have become estranged
  3. from the greater part of our friends
  4. in every land,
  5. for when I came to them
  6. with gentleness and praise
  7. and longing, they grumbled
  8. and acted haughtily. . . .
