English Translation of Letter: DK 333 (alt: 18)

Marina Rustow, [digital geniza document edition].

Recto

  1. I am writing to you, my lord and master, may God prolong your existence and make eternal your glory, from
  2. al-Quds (Jerusalem), may God rebuild it. My health is good, praise and be to God for his
  3. benefactions. God the exalted knows the anxiety I feel at your news and how
  4. troubled I was collecting the debt owed to you.  But God the exalted 
  5. replaces and compensates. But don't let it worry you: I plan to return
  6. to the blessed al-Quds, and the holy one, blessed be he, will not let me perish.
  7. You know, my lord, what Barizillay said: "Let your servant go back
  8. etc. (and let me die in my own town, near the graves of my father and mother, 2 Sam. 19:39)." As far as livelihood is concerned, it's not a matter of willing it to happen. 
  9. You're aware of what the man of God, peace be upon him, said: "The race is not 
  10. won by the swift etc (Nor the battle by the valiant; Nor is bread won by the wise, Nor wealth by the intelligent, Nor favor by the learned. For the time of mischance comes to all." Ecclesiastes 9:11)." You know everything that has been said
  11. about it, may God keep you. At the moment, I am (writing about) something else: 
  12. a debt that Thābit owes me, which would take too long to explain. I collected five
  13. dinars, among them two full dinars, and the rest Sicilian quarter-dinars,
  14. some good, some old and clipped. If I could agree with someone right now
  15. to send them to you, I would send them, since It's not the kind of thing
  16. for which one writes a suftaja. I'd like to send them to al-Arrajānī 
  17. and subtract something from them and take a suftaja for the amount, if a letter from you arrives
  18. to that effect. He said that he has a partnershp (muʿāmala) with R. Avraham b. Yiṣḥaq
  19. ha-Talmid. As for what you said to me about the situation (of Hiba), 
  20. if he were present in al-Quds, I would bring him him and force him 
  21. to withdraw the money for you, and I would collect it from him. But 
  22. he's not here — he's away in Zughrar. I asked someone to write to my lord
  23. the ḥaver R. ʿAlī to help me rescue your property, and also
  24. to my lord Abū Isḥaq Ibrāhīm, masos ha-yeshiva, may God protect him.
  25. So please wait for whatever will happen and I'll keep you posted. I also wrote
  26. to my lord Abū Isḥaq Barhūn son of Moshe he-ḥaver, may he rest in peace, 

Recto - right margin

  1. to the same effect, may God the exalted support you. Enclosed in this letter

Verso

  1. is a letter to my lord Abū l-Faraj Hiba al-Kohen, may God protect him.
  2. Please give it to him. If he's in the Rīf, please send it
  3. to him. Do not put off writing to me about everything that's creating stress (?).
  4. Perhaps my lord Abū l-Faraj Hibba can help you collect 
  5. the dinars. Answer me immediately. Everyone greets you with
  6. best wishes and misses you. May God answer you with wellbeing
  7. and may you be well, may your household be well, and may everything you have be well.
  8. I received word that ʿImrān is putting the memory of our lord the gaʾon 
  9. Shelomo, may the memory of the righteous be a blessing and have eternal life, in a bad light. What happened to him? He should alter his foul
  10. path. "Also, mold is scattered over him, but he has taken no notice (Hosea 7:9)." What more can I say
  11. about that? A covenant of peace. 

Verso - address

  1. To my lord Abū Yaʿqūb Isḥaq b. Yaʿqūb the ḥaver
  2. al-Maqdisī
  3. may God prolong his existence and make [his glory] and support eternal
  4. from Ḥayyim b. Salām
  5. Fustat, if God [wills]
  6. may it arrive in the Dār al-Ṣarf
