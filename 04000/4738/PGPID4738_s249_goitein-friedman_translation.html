English Translation of Letter: T-S Misc.25.103

S. D. Goitein and Mordechai Akiva Friedman, India traders of the middle ages : documents from the Cairo Geniza : India book (Leiden and Boston: Brill, 2008), vol. 3.

Recto

  1. In Your name, O Merciful.

  2. You will walk, without breaking stride; when you run, you will not stumble.

  3. And Shaddai be your treasure, and precious silver for you.

  4. For the Lord will be your trust; He will keep your feet from being caught. 

  5.  The letters of your excellency the illustrious Sheikh, my lord, have arrived—may God prolong

  6. your  life and make permanent your honored position, rank, loftiness

  7. excellence, highness and ascendancy. May He never deprive

  8. your dwelling-place and court of any good. May He protect

  9. all He has conferred upon you. May He subdue those that are envious of you

  10. and crush your enemies. I was glad when I looked at your letter, and then I read it

  11. full of happiness. “It was like water on a thirsty land” (Prov. 25:25 and Ps. 143:6).

  12. “I was like one that finds much spoil” (cf. Ps. 119:162). It was reassuring for me to learn from it

  13. about your well-being and your satisfactory state. Then I praised God for

  14. this very much and asked Him to give you more of all the best

  15. in His mercy. I, your servant, have taken note of your remark,

  16. my lord, of how little I had written you, my lord, last

  17. year. I, your servant, wish to inform you that I had (sent) {lit., ‘written’} letters

  18. with Sheikh Abū ʿAbd Allāh b. Abu ’l-Katāʾib,

  19. and a consignment as well, to you, my lord, from me, your servant, in the

  20. Jurbattanī, the ship of the Sultan. You know,

  21. my master, what happened to it. Thus, the letters

  22. and the consignment entrusted to him were lost. In the ship

  23. in which Sheikh Abū Zikrī Ibn al-Shāmī traveled (I had sent) letters to notify you

  24. about the consignment sent with Sheikh Abū ʿAbd Allah b. Abu ’l-

  25. Katāʾib. However, this ship, too, suffered what

  26. you know. For I, your servant, enquired about you,

  27. my lord, and learned {lit., ‘and they told me’} that your house and your children were in

  28. Jurbattan. Therefore, I sent the things with the above mentioned. 

  29. As to the news concerning your brother Mevasser—he is well, but has not arrived.

  30. I shall act in this matter {alt. tr.: concerning him}, as you advised, my lord. Concerning

  31. the forty dinars transmitted by Sheikh

  32. Maḍmūn through Sheikh Abū Naṣr b. Elisha,

  33. I have no information; I do not know whether he (Mevasser) received something

  34. or not. There arrived what you, my lord, kindly sent, namely the three 

  35. qaṣa-bowls, the water skin with lemon, the ginger, and the basket

  36. with pepper and ginger. I received all this and thank

  37. you, my lord, for your kindness and effort in this matter.

  38. May God reward you, my master, and satisfy your needs {alt. tr.: undertake your compensation}, and

  39. may I never {lit., ‘may He never let me’} miss you! As to excusing you, my lord, for not being able to purchase the new

  40. betel nuts—you, my lord,

  41. are excused, for all the travelers 

  42. arrived and told the same story. I, your servant, sent

  43. with Sheikh Abū ʿAlī Ibn al-Ḥalla to

  44. Manj<ar>ūr two bottles of sugar, two bottles of raisins, and a bottle

  45. of almonds, a total of five bottles. The bottle with almonds was  filled

  46. with sugar, for it had a little empty space; therefore, I filled it

  47. with sugar. I, your servant, sent you also with the above

  48. mentioned (Abū ʿAlī) half a pound of vitriol, half a pound of chewing gum,

  49. ten sheets of white paper and a pound of costus.

  50. I could not get hold of arsenic in

  51. the market; otherwise, I would have sent it to you, my lord {alt. tr.: but it will reach you (later), my lord}. Please, my master,

  52. receive for your esteemed self the best greetings from me.

  53. And to my lords, your children—may God let you enjoy their being alive {alt. tr.: give you pleasure with them throughout your life}!—

  54. and to Sheikh Bama the best greetings.

  55. And your servants, my children, send you

  56. and your children the best greetings. And Peace.

  57. Written in the month of Elul of the year 

  58. 1458 E.D.

Verso

  1. (To) His excellency, the most illustrious Sheikh 
  2. Abū Isḥāq Abraham b. 
  3. Peraḥya, known as Ibn Yijū.
  4. (From) His servant and the property of his hands,
  5. Khalaf b. Isaac—
  6. may God’s spirit give him peace!
