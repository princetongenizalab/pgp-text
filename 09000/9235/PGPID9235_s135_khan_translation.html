English Translation of State document: T-S Ar.30.273

Geoffrey Khan, Arabic Legal and Administrative Documents in the Cambridge Genizah Collections (Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 1993).

Recto

  1. The servant and slave

  1. Mūsā ibn Ṣadaqa, the Jewish merchant.

  2. In the name of God, the merciful and compassionate.

  3. The benedictions of God and his blessings, his increasing benefactions and most excellent peace and greetings be upon our master

  4. and lord, the ’imām of our era and time, the ’imām al-'Amir bi-’Aḥkām Allāh, commander of the faithful, and upon

  5. his pure ancestors and most noble sons, enduring benedictions, which last until the day of judgement. The slave kisses the ground

  6. before the pure and noble prophetical presence, may God double its light and exalt

  7. its beacon, and give victory to its armies, banners and helpers, and reports

  8. that he arrived from India and Yemen with merchandise and (goods or money) in commenda

  9. but it was detained by a pretext on which it was not lawful for it to be detained The slave had registered (documents)

  10. in the court of the qāḍī,i Jalāl al-Mulk Tāj al-’Aḥkām in order to prevent [any dispute].

  1. The slave humbly asks for a kindness to be bestowed upon him and a benefaction granted

  2. to him by the issuing of an exalted rescript, may God increase its efficacy and nobility,

  3. to the qāḍī,i Jalāl al-Mulk Tāj al-’Aḥkām (instructing him) to indicate what the slave has registered

  4. with him, in order that this be a means of restoring his right to him, out of compassion

  5. and commiseration for him. To our lord, the peace of God be upon his name,

  6. belongs the exalted resolution in this, if God wills, he is mighty and glorious.

  7. Praise be to God alone.

  8. The blessings and peace of God be upon our lord Mujlammad the prophet and his family.
