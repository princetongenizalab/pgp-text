English Translation of Letter: ENA 4020.25

Pratima Gopalakrishnan, unpublished.

Recto, top margin

  1. [...] I am short on words.

  2. [The] kindness [you will show] him is part of the kindness you will show

  3. [me.] You [are not] the one who needs to speak about acting kindly (?)

Verso, main text

  1. I informed my lord what Ibn Sutayt and Ibn al-Shimūs Shimūs did to me,

  2. and that is that I had the corals for the elder Abū Zikrī. I agreed to

  3. smuggle it out of customs in ʿAdhāb. The aforementioned proceeded, may they be remembered

  4. in banishment, and reported on me. They lost me 7.5 dīnārs. By my trust 

  5. in Heaven, what happened with me is that I only ever dealt with them charitably, and they dealt with me

  6. with purulence. Evil will never leave the houses of those who repay goodness with wickedness (Proverbs 17:13). 

  7. I am not telling you this, but rather you yourself know the (rank of) the Jews,

  8. by God. (This letter) is written (to you, may God) protect you, from the harbor, as I am boarding the ship

  9. on the 25th day of Iyyār. Please forgive me for any mistakes. Great wellbeing.

  10. I send my lord and master my best, most abundant,

  11. greatest and complete regards. Likewise to my lord the most exalted elder, (our) te(acher) and ma(ster) Yeḥezkel,

Verso, right margin

  1. your brother, may God prolong his life, and the best wellbeing for 

  2. his sons. To the elder Abū Naṣir and the elder Abu ʾl Faraj,

  3. the best wellbeing, and great peace.

Verso, address, right column

  1. [In honor of (his) greatness and holines]s, the excellent and precious, (our) te(acher) and ma(ster)

  2. [Ḥalfon ha-Levi, so]n of his honor (our) te(acher) and ma(ster) Netanʾel

  3. [ha-Levi, m(ay)] (his) s(oul be) b(undled) in the (bundle of life).

 

Verso, addrses, left colum

  1. From his servant,

  2. thanking him for his great

  3. benevolence, Yiṣḥaq

  4. Sijilmāsī.
