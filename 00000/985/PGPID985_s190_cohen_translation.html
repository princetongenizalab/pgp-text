English Translation of Letter: T-S 13J17.4 + T-S 10J10.9

Mark Cohen, The Voice of the Poor in the Middle Ages (Princeton, New Jersey: Princeton University Press, 2005).

Recto

  1. This man was one of the notables of his community in his place, and he was rich.
  2. The ruler of his land imposed a punishment upon him, so he fled from there
  3. to Damascus. Then he left Damascus for the
  4. land of Israel, but brigands attacked him, looting all (he had)
  5. and injuring him. He is in p[a]in. When he reached the Holy
  6. City, he said, "I shall go to Egypt where they will perhaps give me [s]omething
  7. for the road so I can I return to my place." It is the haver's
  8. habit, (may your) R(ock) p(rotect you), to be good to all who come to
  9. Egypt, so may you be so with this man. Be his intercessor
  10. and advocate and speak to our elders
  11. in the holy community, those who are in the synagogue and those who
  12. are at home. May the haver give him proper advice so that he may return
  13. to his home. May the honored King reward you. May the haver inform
  14. the community about the power of chari[t]y that is given to those who regularly stretch forth their hands,
  15. all the more so to those who have f[allen] from their [w]ealth. I await the arrival
  16. of your letter about what is being done for him and also about your situation
  17. and every affair and every need (cf. Daniel 3:16). May your welfare increase, and regards
  18. to all the admirers and benefactors, the e[lders] and the cantor and the entire
  19. community [ ... 4]795 [1034-35].
