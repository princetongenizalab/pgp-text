English Translation of State document: ENA 4020.65

S. D. Goitein, "Congregation versus Community: An Unknown Chapter in the Communal History of Jewish Palestine," Jewish Quarterly Review 44, no. 4 (Philadelphia, PA: University of Pennsylvania Press, 1954), 291-304.

Recto

  1. And as far as the argument in favor of the two synagogues and the two authorities is concerned, (it has already been refuted by) Ibn

  2. Sijilmasi, for when two authorities are permissible, there would be permissible

  3. three and even more, and this would lead to endless anarchy,

  4. to the robbing of land and goods, to the rape of women and the end of rights.

  5. The benefaction bestowed (on me) then would be no benefaction at all, and it would be impossible to get even from the simplest

  6. man what was due from him.

It is the law of our religion that there should be no authority above

  1. the authority of Jerusalem, for the Holy City is the place towards which all (Jews) turn in prayer, and God, may He be exalted, has ordained

  2. that the commands of the Head who will be in Jerusalem shall be obeyed and his leadership 

  3. followed, and that he who disobeys him, disobeys God. This is expressly stated

  4. in their Torah. And just as the body cannot exist with two heads, so there is

  5. no learning in a town with two rabbis. These synagogues of the Iraqis are attributed

  6. to them, because they have the custom of keeping a second day after

  7. they keep with us a holiday. This was permitted to them by the Heads of Jerusalem

  8. as a special favor so that they could pray there on the day of their holiday, which is on the day

  9. following our holiday, which they keep together with us, but nothing

  10. else. The proof of the truth of what your servants have said is that in Palestine

  11. there are various places, in which there are synagogues of Iraqis, but these are solely

  12. for prayer, and they have not in them a Judge or any other official. All they are allowed

  13. to do is granted to them as a favor on the side of the Head who is in Jerusalem all the time, for they

  14. come to Jerusalem for the pilgrimage. This affair is a machination

  15. of the partisans of Joseph, who agitate for the leaders of Iraq. For they have connived

  16. with him, that if he should succeed in getting your signature, approving him

  17. a headship, this would strengthen them in what they intended to do for Joseph and his like.

  18. However, our Lord — may God pray upon him and upon his pure (i. e. defunct) fathers — in his farsightedness will

  19. grant the request of his servants to repudiate those people

  20. who try to split up what is bestowed upon your servant; for,

  21. when he will get a partner in this benefaction, it will become a vexation. The pure excellencies (i. e. the defunct notables) have made grants

  22. in many letters to many Heads in the course of time — the archives

  23. testify to this — but they have never given to anyone of them a partner, in what they bestowed

  24. upon them, for the letter of the Government — may God bestow glory upon its victories — is laid down ... 

A(nd the well-being of your) H(ighness) m(ay increase), n(ot) d(ecrease); and Peace!
