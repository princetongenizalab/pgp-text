English Translation of State document: T-S Ar.40.16

Geoffrey Khan, Arabic Legal and Administrative Documents in the Cambridge Genizah Collections (Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 1993).

  1. The slave

  2. Makārim ibn Isḥāq, the doctor.

  3. In the name of God, the merciful and compassionate.

  4. (The slave) kisses the ground and reports that he is a trained and able practitioner in the profession

  5. of medicine. The two doctors of our master the sultan, al-Rashīd al-Dimashqī andal-Rashīd

  6. Abū Ḥulayqa, attest to the excellence of the slave's knowledge in this profession. He makes

  7. a request before our lord the sultan, may God make great his victory, of the gift

  8. of the remainder of the salary of al-Asʿad, the doctor in the hospital of Cairo,

  9. that is three dīnārs every month, (paid) to the slave for him to practise

  10. the treatment of the sick and let the prayer (of the slave) [for] our lord the sultan be answered. The resolution (of the sultan) is exalted. 

  11. Praise be to God alone and his blessings [be upon our lord] Muḥammad and his family and save them.
