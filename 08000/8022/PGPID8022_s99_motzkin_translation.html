English Translation of Legal document: T-S 13J22.7

Motzkin, "The Arabic Correspondence of Judge Elijah and his Family" (PhD diss., n.p., 1965), 2.

Recto

  1. I (would like to) inform the august,

  2. exalted and gracious presence of our master

  3. and teacher Elijah the enlightened,

  4. wise and understanding judge - (may his) R(ock) p(reserve him). - Now, then.

  5. Your maid-servant, the wife of the al-Sadi'd

  6. departed from you grateful

  7. for your graciousness and kindness. I am still

  8. without news from you

  9. about the boy. I wish you would inform me

  10. how he is and what he is doing.

  11. I only refrained from coming out of fear

  12. that the boy be seized on account of the poll-tax,

  13. and I would be punished for him. You know the heart

  14. of a mother, and that I cannot enjoy life

  15. because of being separated from him. I ask

  16. your grace that you send me some of the money

  17. which is kept by you. For I am in terrible straits

  18. on account of the depression and I have nothing at all. The children

  19. need something of this (money), while we have nothing

  20. except God and you. Don't force me

  21. to come (to Cairo). Take heed! Take heed!

Top Margin

  1. Umm Makin

  2. Bilbays

Verso

  1. Abu Zikri

  2. Abu al-Faraj

  3. The New Street
