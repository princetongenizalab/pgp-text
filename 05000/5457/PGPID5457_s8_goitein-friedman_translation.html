English Translation of Letter: T-S AS 154.116 + T-S 24.66

S. D. Goitein and Mordechai Akiva Friedman, India traders of the middle ages : documents from the Cairo Geniza : India book (Leiden and Boston: Brill, 2008), vol. 1.

recto

  1. ...] in His mercy. I noted that you mentioned
  2. in your esteemed letter
  3. that you had sent some pepper in the ship of the nākhudā Rāmisht
  4. —twelve bahārs of small measure. This has arrived 
  5. and (I) your servant went to collect it. From this is to be deducted
  6. —as you mention in your letter—forty
  7. -five pounds, leaving you eleven
  8. bahārs and two hundred and fifty-five pounds, the price being
  9. 34 dinars (per bahār). The (total) value:
  10. 403 dinars, minus one-sixth.
  11. From this (sum) is to be deducted the ‘tithe,’ 82 ¼ 
  12. dinars; the cost for taking delivery of the goods, 4 ⅙ dinars;
  13. the cost of baskets of palm leaves and a porter, one and one-quarter and one-sixth dinars;
  14. a total of 88 dinars minus one-sixth,
  15. leaving 315 dinars (in your favor).
  16. You mentioned that you sent in the ship of the nākhudā
  17. Abu ʾl-Ḥasan b. Abu ʾl-Katāʾib some refurbished
  18. iron—twenty-one bahārs. But
  19. the nākhudā Abū ʿAbd Allah, his son, gave me only
  20. seventeen bahārs of large measure; he stated that
  21. the Bānyān (the Indian merchant), whom you asked to take charge of {alt. tr.: to whom you referred him for collection of} the iron,
  22. had delivered no more than this to him, saying that the rest of
  23. the iron was in the highlands and had not yet arrived. In reply, I held him (Abū ʿAbd Allah) to be under obligation {alt. tr.: I bound him by a stipulation},
  24. if this were not true,
  25. to pay the price (of it), according to the sale value in Aden.
  26. He is to pay this to you, my lord, in India.
  27. The iron I received from you in Aden is twenty
  28. bahārs and one hundred and twenty pounds of small measure.
  29. From this is to be deducted: for the elder Joseph b.
  30. Abraham, three bahārs and one hundred and eighty
  31. pounds; for Khalaf b. Isḥāq, two bahārs
  32. and one-quarter—a total of five bahārs and two hundred
  33. and fifty-five pounds, leaving you four-
  34. teen bahārs and one hundred and sixty-five pounds,
  35. the price being 17 dinars (per bahār). The (total) value:
  36. 247 ¼ dinars. From this is to be deducted
  37. the ‘tithe,’ the expenses {alt. tr.: toll}, and the (cost of) the porter,
  38. 27 ¼ dinars, leaving 220
  39. dinars. Therefore, the sum total owed you is (approximately)
  40. 535 dinars. I (your servant) bought you
  41. three bags of copper, weighing
  42. five bahārs, at a cost of 83 (dinars per bahār).
  43. The (total) value: 415 dinars.
  44. The number of pieces in each bag is twenty-three. 
  45. The cost of hides and packing: 1 ½ dinars. Exit tolls
  46. from the Furḍa, 4 ⅛ dinars. I sent you
  47. this in the ship of the nākhudā Rāmisht,
  48. one bag; in the ship of al-Muqaddam, one bag; and in the ship of
  49. Nambiyar (ani?) {Read: Nmby Rwy}, one bag—a total of three bags. Freight charges for 
  50. this (were) 4 ½ dinars. (Also) charged to you, the registration fee from {alt. tr.: of} the 
  51. ship’s captain for the pepper and the iron, two dinars.
