English Translation of Letter: Bodl. MS heb. d 66/61

S. D. Goitein and Mordechai Akiva Friedman, India traders of the middle ages : documents from the Cairo Geniza : India book (Leiden and Boston: Brill, 2008), vol. 3.

  9.  Your servant {lit., ‘the property of his hands’} took notice
  10. of your inquiry, my lord, about the
  11. news of the roads and the reconciliation between the Dā'ī
  12. and his adversaries.
  13. I am able to inform you, my lord,
  14. that even one or two persons can travel on any road,
  15. and that the whole country is peaceful. Likewise, the Dā'ī has been reconciled
  16. with all his enemies and has received from them 
  17. hostages. All the ships have arrived
  18. safely. Sheikh Maḍmūn—may God remember him favorably {alt. tr: may he be remembered for a blessing!}—has seized
  19. the entire two shops, and nothing remains in the possession of Ibrahīm
  20. except the doorway and the room for the manufacture of wine. This was done out of compassion
  21. by him (Maḍmūn) for the people. Now I pray to the God of Israel to protect you
  22. from all misfortunes and calamities. Please
  23. do not ask me, my lord, what I experienced from Mūsā
  24. b. Mevōrākh and his malicious joy at my misfortune over the loss
  25. of my shop. Yes, my shop was taken away from me by force {alt. tr.: I was robbed only of my shop}.

Margin

  1. Receive for your honored self the best
  2. greetings, and do not defer your travel—may God assist you!—
  3. indeed, to our place. And please, do not travel
  4. except in the company of people on whom you can rely.
  5. May peace for you overtake
  6. peace!
  7. This letter was written in a hurry.
