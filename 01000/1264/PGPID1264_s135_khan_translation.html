English Translation of Legal document: T-S Ar.5.1

Geoffrey Khan, Arabic Legal and Administrative Documents in the Cambridge Genizah Collections (Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 1993).

Verso, left side 

  1. In the name of God, the merciful and compassionate. God bless Muḥammad the  prophet and his family and save them.  

  2. This is what ʿAbd al-Bāqī ibn Aḥmad, the preacher, leased from Mūsā ibn ʿAdī,  the Jew, in one contract: all of the  

  3. house that is in his possession and in his ownership, as a valid asset and a right  with legal force, in the place known as Khawlān in the cul-de-sac street  

  4. know as Muzāḥim street, in the vicinity of the mosque known as al-Abqūr mosque, it being the house with a courtyard,  

  5. a spring water well, two upper dwellings above it and three huts that constitute  upper apartments thereof,  

  6. [the fame] of this house in its location making it unnecessary to define it in this  document. (He leased it) for the period of a complete year, twelve months,  beginning  

  7.  in the middle of Muḥarram of the year four-hundred and twenty-four and ending  in the middle of Muḥarram of the year  

  8. four-hundred and twenty-five, for six dīnārs, assayed, full weight, of good alloy,  on the understanding that this lessee will pay  

  9. this lessor at the end of each month the appropriate instalment of it, that is half a  dīnār, without delaying that or withholding it, everything that  

  10. this lease entailed being free, without encumbrance, in a valid, permissible lease.  This took place after they had seen everything that  

  11. this lease entailed and had recognized it. The lessor handed over to the lessee  everything that this lease entailed and his right  

  12. to use it however he wishes came into force, whether inhabiting it or leasing it.  Whatever repairs are necessary  

  13. in this house are the responsibility of the aforementioned lessee. They also called  witnesses, while being in sound health and legally capable of conducting their  affairs.  

  14. It was written when thirteen days had passed of Muḥarram in the year four-hundred and twenty-four.  

 

Witness Clauses 

  1. Muḥammad ibn Aḥmad ibn ʿAlī ibn .... (valid) testified  

  2. to the acknowledgement by the lessor and the lessee  

  3. of what is contained herein. Valid.  

  4. ʿAlī ibn Ibrāhīm ibn Ḥasan ibn Ḥusayn (valid) testified  

  5. to their acknowledgement of what is contained herein. Valid.  

  6. Aḥmad ibn ʿAbdallāh ibn Muḥammad ibn ʿAbdallāh (valid) testified  

  7. to the acknowledgement by the lessor and the lessee of what is contained herein.
