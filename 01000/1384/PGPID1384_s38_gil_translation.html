English Translation of Letter: T-S Misc.28.52

Moshe Gil, Documents of the Jewish Pious Foundations from the Cairo Geniza (Leiden: Brill, 1976).

Recto, main region

  1. (1-2) .... (your) servant recognizes the wide beneficences and (your) name as benefactor. And may He, His Name be praised, make that (you) our Master
  2.  
  3. (3-4) be solicited rather than a solicitor. Your servant appeals in the matter of a man who verily is a God fearing man and applies for the sake of bringing about what is good and beneficent. Now,
  4.  
  5. it is out of constraint that I bring to your knowledge my  situation, and the way it was handled. Your servant
  6. has lived in the compound that is in the vicinity of the Synagogue, may it be rebuilt and reconstructed, for 13
  7. years, for 12 (dir.) a month. I hold (a deed with) the signature of al-Nāgīd, our Master
  8. David, may His Honor increase, in which he stipulates that I inhabit
  9. it for the specified rent. There is an explanation in it whose formulation is long
  10. (10-11) so that it would take me too long to present it here. To put it briefly, in it he said that it is in the public interest that al-Melammēd should live in the above mentioned compound without any rent at all; and then,
  11.  
  12. due to his critical situation, the heqdēsh will be satisfied with 12 (dir.) a month,
  13. to ease his distress with, and (to enable him) to watch these few children of Israel

Recto, margin

  1. with (the stave of) kindness, āmēn, sella. And may the Master accept the apology of his servant.
