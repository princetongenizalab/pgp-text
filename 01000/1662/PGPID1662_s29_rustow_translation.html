English Translation of Letter: T-S 12.383

Marina Rustow, [digital geniza document edition].

Recto

  1. In the name of God, the merciful, the compassionate.
  2. I am writing, my lord and master — may God prolong your existance, make eternal your glory, smite your enemies, protect me 
  3. from all evil, redeem you and inscribe you for wellbeing and health, praise be to God, lord of the universe — longing for you, may God bring us together
  4. in the happiest of circumstances in his benificence. Your letter arrived, my lord, at the moment when they set sail (Goitein: the Nile ships) after
  5. they had said it was lost. I took it so hard that when it arrived, I felt no relief. I attached no importance to ...
  6. You mentioned that the letter didn't reach you until Sunday at noon. This is the deed of Farjūn, may God banish
  7. his name and cut short his days (on earth), since he's the one who craeted this situation for me. When they (the ships) arrived
  8. on Friday at noon, he took the letter, but If only he had let them alone, curse him (al-malʿūn), to work in their usual fashion (ʿalā rasmihim). But instead, he
  9. wanted to conduct his dealings as if he were working together with me until he loaded his goods — may God curse him today and every day!
  10. You wrote that some of the honey was missing, but you did not write which jars were full and which were less full — full to the brim
  11. like the previous ones, up to their necks. I asked you to inform me whether the sacks containing the wax
  12. that was sent with Qūrīl had five pieces of wax or not. Please sell everything however you can (at whatever price you can obtain).
  13. Don't neglect anything. As for what my lord mentioned on the matter of the mulḥam (cloth), the [ ], and the rest of it, we spoke about this at length, and (now) you're coming back
  14. to it. I would buy it for a dirham and sell it for three.

Verso

  1. You mention that Farjūn, may God curse him, indicated that you should not buy it. I swear by my life, he is an expert in trade?!
  2. The ship's crew didn't delay me at all. They packed in my things and were done with it, so don't worry. Let me know
  3. if the first jars of honey were full up to the neck and the latter ones up to their heads. Please, my lord, sell this 
  4. wax and honey and the ..., and let me know the accounting and the quantity. I read the finest greeetings to my lord.
  5. If money comes in, send this account, the other (accounts), the sacks, the locks, and ream (dast) of Damascus paper and the good, if possible.

Verso - address

  1. [To] my lord and master Abū Bishr and Abū Yaʿaqov, may God lengthen their existence and make their glory eternal, 
  2. Yaʿaqov ibn Yūsuf and Yūsuf ibn Yaʿaqov. From Yiṣḥaq Ibn Janūn.
