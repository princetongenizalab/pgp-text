English Translation of Letter: T-S 13J14.14

Marina Rustow, [digital geniza document edition].

Recto

  1. In your name, merciful one.
  2. May God the exalted listen to what his servant implores his excellency our lord
  3. his honor, greatness and holiness, our master and teacher Mevorakh, sagacious leader, sage of the yeshiva, 
  4. grand counsel, strength of the house of Israel, prince of princes, nagid
  5. of nagids, may his glory be exalted and his honor be magnified, son of his honor, greatness and holiness our master and [teacher]
  6. Saʿadya the physician, pious one of his generation, may the memory of the righteous be a blessing, and may the two honored princes live,
  7. at the head and the beginning, his honor, greatness and holiness our master and teacher the rayyis Abū l-Bayān
  8. Moshe, and may the rayyis Abū l-Barakāt live. The servant has no tongut
  9. to convey supplication and thanks for even some of the benefactions that his excellency has lavished
  10. on his servant. I am sitting among my children, not having ? from God
  11. and from some of his benefactions upon me. An appointment was validated among all the traders 
  12. who have obtained a tax-farm for his servant, and his collecting money and refu[sing] 
  13. me since I wasn't one of the groups called the Banū Ḥujayla from Tyre. A[mong them]
  14. is the evil sinner. They hold the slave in contempt, since they have a taste for bribery, may they be cursed.
  15. They are scrutinizing me and everything I could add to their esteem, they covet in me.
  16. The Banū Ḥujayla have started mentioning that they are outbidding me in the dīwān,
  17. and saying that they have a (Jewish) judge with them who is a cavalryman and a Qaraite, Ibn Bayrūtī, and that 
  18. al-Dhukhayra and Ibn Abī Sāma look after them. So they say. They went to

Recto, right margin

  1. the tax-farm that is in
  2. Minya, of which I am the tax-farmer
  3. rather than them. They outbid me, and didn't
  4. accept a greater (offer). They said to them: 
  5. This tax-farmer (received his appointment) from 
  6. Dhū al-Riʾāsatayn for three
  7. years, and h[e has]
  8. one and a half remaining. Let us not accept 
  9. a greater offer until he finishes (the term of his) tax-farm
  10. and oath (?). Were it not for the fact that I don't
  11. owe the fiscal officials (muʿāmilīn) any debts,
  12. I wouldn't have troubled the prince
  13. our lord, may his glory be exalted.

Recto, top margin

  1. The servant awaits his recompense
  2. from God and from his excellency,
  3. and my redemption from the people of this group
  4. who are ruining me. If they keep 
  5. from me this year and a half without
  6. surplus, then (I will be dependent) on God and his excellency.
  7. And if they ..., then the slave
  8. will accept it since I owe debts
  9. to the traders, and they are killing me. I beg
  10. God and his grace to bestow upon me
  11. as he has bestowed on me before and
  12. forever. Amen let it be His will.
