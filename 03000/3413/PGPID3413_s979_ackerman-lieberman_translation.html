English Translation of Legal document: T-S 16.11

Phillip Ackerman-Lieberman, "A Partnership Culture: Jewish Economic and Social Life Seen Through the Legal Documents of the Cairo Geniza" (PhD diss., Princeton University, 2007).

Recto

  1. … forty […]
  2. who is known as Ibn Zaffān since he was […] and now, “Testify before me and perform a qinyan […]
  3. using all the appropriate legal formulae and [the] expressions of certainty, and all the language used for claims, and give that
  4. to his son //M(r.)// Ḥalfōn, known as Abū [Sa‘ī]d, and to his mother and his sister, and to all the heirs of this Moses (whose) s(oul is at) r(est) b. Ḥalfōn,
  5. so that they would have in their hands proof and a legal claim from this day forward. This was according to my free will, not under duress norunder compulsion nor
  6. any of the like from all the factors which invalidate testimony, rather with a whole heart and a desirous s[ou]l, I
  7. attest before you that I absolve this Moses, (who) r(ests in) E(den), from the financial transactions (mu‘āmalāt)between me and him
  8. and from the shipments mentioned above, and from my partnership (khulṭa) with the non-Jew and from the like; and from all that to which he could turn
  9. and from all that which passed //between us// from the beginning of time up till the day of his death, may it be an atonement for him and for their house, and for all [Israel]
  10. and I have released him completely from all the like, both with oral testament and in (my) heart, for all that I know and that which has been hidden from me.
  11. Likewise, I absolve M(r.) Ḥalfōn—known as Abū Sa‘īd—his son, and his mother who was the wife of  
  12. this Moses, (may he) r(est in Eden). And I absolve him and all of the descendants of this M(r.) Moses (may his) s(oul find) r(est), from all the claims and demands
  13. concerning these shipments of merchandise mentioned above as well as others, from the part (that is, the responsibility) of M(r.) Moses (who) r(ests in) E(den) and from their part
  14. with a total, complete absolution, severing all claims and demands in an irreversible absolution,
  15. immune to argument and not subject to any interpretation weakening it, an absolution from me—me, Ephraim b. Ḥalfōn—in my lifetime and from my heirs after
  16. my death, from every debt and matter from clear claims and possible claims, and from any matter which could be called “financial”, and from (the claim of) overreaching
  17. and grievance (claims), within the earthly jurisdiction and the Heavenly jurisdiction, and from oaths and decrees and from rider oaths and pronouncements,
  18. both general and specific, and from the determination of any court, whether in Jewish law or gentile law. I have released them from no[w on] from
  19. all negligence or omission, proceeding from them from the beginning of time up till now, with a complete irreversible release
  20. for all generations. I, Ephraim b. Ḥalfōn, have no (right of) reversal of claim(s) upon the descendants of this //M(r.)// Moses in the name of
  21. anything at all from that which proceeded between me and this M(r.) Moses, m(ay his) s(oul find rest), nor in that which proceeded between me and them in
  22. their own possessions from the beginning of time up till now, since nothing remains with me on their part, nor is there on the part of
  23. their heirs any sort of claim, neither a legacy, nor a dinar nor a dirham nor merchandise
  24. nor a guarantee nor a security, nor an account nor an accounting error nor the remainder of an account, nor a legal judgment, nor anything whatsoever from
  25. all matters which people may possess under all the Heavens equivalent to or greater than a farthing. Thus, they are freed
  26. from all types of oaths. They are completely free and completely released (from any obligation), both by means of oral testament and in (my) heart, in this world and in the world to come.
  27. I took that responsibility upon myself for them, and the responsibility of the non-Jew who was my partner in the cloth. I have also nullified all
  28. secret dispositions and conditions which may be made for me and which we have made in connection with this document of release, which is as established by the Sa(ges), (may their) m(emory be) f(or a blessing), not like promises
  29. and not like formularies, rather but rather (bears) the weight and force of all legal documents of the court, which the Sages instituted, and which are universally employed
  30. from this day forward. We, the witnesses, performed a qinyan with Ephraim b. Ḥalfōn and with M(r.) Ḥalfōn b. Moses m(ay his) s(oul find rest), and with all his descendants
  31. concerning all that which is written and specified above, with an item suitable for doing so. Proper and enduring. Hanging (between the lines): “that which was between us”
  32. Moses ha-Kohen b. Aaron, (who) r(ests in) E(den)  Manasseh ha-Kohen b. Japheth (may his) s(oul find) r(est)    Hanging (between the lines): “Enduring and this is its confirmation”   We do not rely on that which is in the (other) authoritative document, or (derives) from it. //It is enduring// and this is its confirmation.
  33. Moses b. Solomon m(ay his) s(oul find rest)     Yeshu‘a b. Shemariah m(ay his) s(oul find rest)
